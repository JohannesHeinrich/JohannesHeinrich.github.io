Acute Chronic Workload Ratio / Chronic Intensity Load

Chronic Intensity Load, CIL (1st/half)

A newer parameter to measure Training Load based on the Intensity Factor. The Chronic Intensity Factor, CIL, tells how hard a session was relative to the threshold power. Intensity Factor is an option to measure training stress exclusive of duration as length of a training session is not always key to success. The CIL is not a replacement for the CTL it complements.

  • Above Red Line: Indicates a relative high intensity
  • Between Red and Green Line: Bandwidth for rather normal training intensities
  • Below Green Line: Indicates a relative low intensity

Acute Chronic Workload Ratio, ACWR (2nd/half)

The Acute Chronic Workload Ratio, ACWR, shall be used to help predicting and indicating injury risks by calculating the ratio between Acute Chronic Workload, performed over the past 7 days, and the Chronic Training Load.

  • If Acute Chronic Workload is higher in relation to the Chronic Training Load, it indicates too much load than normally done, thus creating a ‘spike for stimulation’ in training load. Those peaks serve as warning signs to get closer to increase risks of injuries.
  • If Acute Chronic Workload is lower than the Chronic Training Load, it indicates potential detraining which may lead to an increased risk of injury, as the body is not prepared for higher training demands.
  • Acute Chronic Workload Ratio, ACWR, ranging from 0.8 to 1.3 seems to be the sweet spot for low risk of injury and still steady training progress.
  • At the end this is highly individual, as each athlete is completely different.
