Training Load - ATL, CTL & TSB

You´re in training planning private business or training planning business business now. This means it´s important to understand the basic KPIs behind.

allemagne Good articles to start reading for German speaking athletes:
Pushing Limits, Jürgen Pansy erklärens ganz gut, was es ist.

english Fair starting points for non German speaking athletes:
Trainer Road, Trainingspeaks explain it quite well what´s it about.

You make your own a favour if you spend some more time to look deeper into this topic. Really, very important.

I can adjust the basic parameters in #mytraininplanning:

  • ATL “Acute Training Load assumes fatigue from the most recent training load”
  • CTL “Chronic Training Load reflects fitness which is built over a longer period of time”
  • TSB “Training Stress Balance predicts form to become race day ready”

Additional I also want to reflect historical learnings for future peak performance and training zones indication:

  • “Peak Performance Lower&Upper Limit” Sets the lower&upper limit of your individual peak performance.
  • “Training Zone Lower Limit” Sets the lower&upper limits of your individual training zone.

Estimator & Predictor

What´s in the book is in the books. We can´t change it. But we can learn from it and derive predictions to improve with upcoming training session. This is what´s in a nutshell behind the basic estimator and the predictor. The latter case is automatically on the table once enough good historical data is at hand. Nerds would call it now Machine Learning or Artificial Intelligence. For me it´s just pretty awesome and sophisticated backend logic leveraging the best data ever. My own one.

Results and predictions from the estimator and the predictor are mainly used in the planning mode for upcoming sessions and the impact on the training load.
